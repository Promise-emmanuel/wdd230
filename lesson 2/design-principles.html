Alignment

Freshy sites

In design, the principles of alignment refers to the way the different elements in a design are lined up or arranged in relation to each other or to a particular part of the page. The elements can be centered, right-aligned or left-aligned relative to other items in the design.
In this example, we see several items aligned centrally to the webpage, the headings, the "Get in touch or get a quote" button link and also the image showing different devices are all aligned and have enough white space between each other.

Constrast

Zenith Bank

Constrast simply means determining what the first thing is that you want the viewer to look at. It is designing a distinct difference between the foreground and background colors in order to enhance readability and user interaction.
In this example, we see some buttons such as "open account" and "learn more" having a background color of "red". The constrast is there to persuade users to click on them so they begin to immediately interact with the page.

Visual Hierachy

Apples

Visual Hierachy is the systematic arrangement of the elements in a webpage according to their order of importance.
Visual Hierachy is used to rank elements in the order you want your users to view them.
In this example, we see a bold heading followed by the links to buy the product and then the image of the product all arranged systematically.
